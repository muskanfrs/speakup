Error Notebook

Your mistakes are saved here so you can learn from them — not feel bad about them.

She don't know the answer.
✓ She doesn't know the answer.
He / She / It → always use "doesn't", not "don't".
I am knowing her since 2020.
✓ I have known her since 2020.
"Know" is a state verb — never use it in the -ing form.
Yesterday I go to the market.
✓ Yesterday I went to the market.
Use past tense for completed actions. "Go" becomes "went".
Can you explain me this?
✓ Can you explain this to me?
"Explain" needs "to" before the person: explain X to Y.
I have did my work.
✓ I have done my work.
After "have/has", use the past participle — "done", not "did".
